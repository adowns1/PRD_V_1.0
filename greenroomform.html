Ashley Downs MS, RDN, LDN, CPT

Your Wellness Guide
A Bit About Me

I love rompers and leather jackets.
I will always say yes to a drop in dance class.
I love to play my ukulele and find new ways to turn trash into crafts.
I feel alive playing a juicy part in a play.

Professional

I have worked as a dietitian and personal trainer in various settings.
As a dietitian, I have worked with athletes and designed wellness programs for clinical outpatient settings.
My Master's Thesis was on the nutrition habits of collegiate performing artists.

Education

I earned both my BS and MS in Nutriton and Dietetics from Saint Louis University.
After I started working as a clinical dietitian, I felt like something was missing.
I found that missing piece in acting and earned my BFA in Acting from Lindenwood University Belleville.

Our Vision

Successful performing artists performing to the best of their ability every performance.
I treat performing artists like the artist athletes they are.

Our Mission

To empower you to make informed wellness decisions through live lessons, 1:1 guidance, and practicing as a group.

Our Values

  • All Quadrants, All Levels:
    We value the whole. This includes the interior individual, the exterior individual, the interior community and the exterior community. We act in ways that consider the whole and meet everyone where they are at.

  • Meet and Exceed:
    Expectations matter. Results matter. We deliver on our promises and we don’t make promises we can’t keep.

  • Dare to Differ:
    Just because “that’s how it is” doesn’t mean “that’s how it has to be.”
    We’re open to criticism and change.
    We don’t do the same old song and dance, we dance to our own beat.
    We value other beats as much as our own.

  • Commitment and Respect:
    We respect you.
    We are committed to serving you.
    We care about your journey and are honored to be a part of it.
Our Philosophies



  • Your art is your business. Your wellness is your income.
    You need to be well to perform well.
    Getting sick costs you money.



  • Dismantle Diet Culture.
    Diet culture never did you any favors.
    Don't just avoid it, destroy it.



  • Move the audience, not the scale.
    There is more to wellness than weight loss.
    You can achieve wellness without focusing on weight.
